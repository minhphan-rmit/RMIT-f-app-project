Project description

Overview

Topic

Concerns regarding nutritional imbalances and the growth in obesity rates are widespread. In 1985, no U.S. state had obesity rates higher than 15%, and by 2016, no state had rates lower than 20%, according to Shattuck (2017). The global obesity rate is currently around 33% [1]. It goes without saying that society will gain from addressing nutritional imbalances. Obesity is on the rise while physical activity rates have fallen (Skinner et al., 2018, cited in Armstrong, 2019) [2]. This regulation applies to all IT professionals. People who eschew outdoor activities and spend a lot of time sitting still are more likely to be overweight or obese.

As a result, the subject of nutritional imbalance caught our group's attention. One of the most popular and useful ways to quantify obesity is via BMI. People can readily ascertain their current physical condition by dividing their weight by their height square. Our team found issues with various BMI calculators. This was the problem with merely providing the essential information, such as the current condition and statistics. Our group felt that it would be more useful to make recommendations for the forms of management that are necessary in addition to BMI calculation. We therefore decided to create a calculator that eliminates the need for users to check their BMI, look up workouts online, or locate a different calculator in order to determine how many calories they consumed today. Our technology allows users to calculate their daily calorie intake and receive recommendations depending on their BMI. Users can use this to generally determine if they should raise their diet, exercise more, or lower their diet.

[1]Shattuck, M. 2017, Control diet, weight in diabetes fight: As rates of obesity increase, so too do rates of Type 2 diabetes, Appleton, Wis.

[2]Armstrong, S. & Wong, C. (2019) Physical Activity Interventions Improve Quality of Life of Children With Obesity: Should We Expect Them to Also Decrease Body Mass Index? Journal of adolescent health. [Online] 65 (3), 309–310.

Motivation

After the COVID-19 pandemic and a long period of working from home, people started realizing how important it is to be healthy, both physically and mentally. Based on that realization, we came up with the idea of a virtual assistant for health care. This project can change the habits of the majority of people about their awareness of their health and help people have a better and healthier life.

Smartphones have become an indispensable part of our lives in the past 20 years; everything now can be accessed easily using smartphones, from payment, transportation, socialization,... That is why we came up with a cross-platform mobile virtual assistant that is available on your mobile devices such as smartphones, laptops, tablets,... We believe that the trend of mobile applications will last for many years, and even when augmented reality and virtual reality become popular in the near future, it will be an opportunity for us to make our virtual assistant more "realistic."

This project is not just a "school project" or normal group project. This project is a replication of real-life IT-related projects. As a result, working on this project brings us many benefits. Firstly, we have a chance to work in a professional environment. We do documents like proposals, reports, meeting minutes, papers, etc.; we do academic research for our project idea; we have to obey different work ethics from the normal projects. Secondly, we learn how to work effectively in a group, a department, or something similar. We learn how to stay in touch with people, teammates, and other departments; we learn how to communicate professionally;... In conclusion, there are many invisible benefits that we can gain from working on a project, like technical skills and knowledge aside from professional workflow. This is a good opportunity for us to prove to the employers that we can fit into their business.

Landscape

Our project idea is not highly distinguishable from those on the market. There are many similar products with similar functions to ours. We chose two products that already exist to give an overview of similar products:

  • Nike Training Club is a fitness app from Nike, Inc.
  • We Do Pulse is a personal health assistant made by PULSE ECOSYSTEM PTE. LTD.

Both products mentioned above have almost the same features as our apps. But these products are consistently receiving bad reviews because of a bad user experience. The common problems of the competitors are:

  • The free plans and programs are completely useless.
  • The programs provided are too general and not personalized enough for every user.
  • The User Interface of the app is not attractive and easy-to-use.
  • Most of the time, the users will feel “lonely” because the assistant they have is not really an assistant.

Understanding the users’ problems, our aim is to solve all the problems mentioned above. Besides providing programs through computer calculation and maybe AI in the far future, we are planning to provide a platform to connect trainers and users. This feature will make our product more user-friendly and customizable. Furthermore, we are focusing on developing UX and UI together so as to make the app more easy-to-use and attractive.

Detailed description

Aims

BBased on the disadvantages of other similar products that we mentioned above, we can easily define our most important aim for this project:

A “real” virtual trainer

Being fit and healthy does not mean that you have to work hard like a real body builder. They are usually simple exercises done at home, such as a morning trek in a nearby park, but you may occasionally lose your track; you may not know where to begin or continue your track. This is when a virtual trainer comes in handy. A virtual trainer not only provides you with a training program but also tracks your progress, giving you personalized advice based on your real status.

In order to be a “real” virtual trainer, we have defined some goals for our process of development:

The most attractive UI

For us, UI is one of the most important parts of a mobile app's success. An attractive and energetic UI is a big motivation for our users. Looking at and using an app that is colorful and motivating gives our users more encouragement to work out and stay healthy.

The best UX

Building a system that has a good user experience is a big challenge for every project. There are two main points on which we will focus on the UX of our product: simplify the way of using the app by easing the connection between users and trainers; and personalize the app as much as possible for every user so that they will feel cared for and motivated.

Verified trainers

Our product is not only for users who want to stay healthy but also for trainers who are passionate about bodybuilding and willing to share their knowledge to help other people stay healthy. This will help them develop their passion and provide them with a source of income.

Testing

Our group made an effort not to have a big bug or problem after the release. We expected that finding and resolving bugs during the development process would increase development and operational efficiency rather than creating bugs after our project was released. We referred to the "Agile development cycle" to make thorough software.

The "Agile development cycle" was chosen by our group because it necessitates continuous interaction and debugging.In addition, these short-period interactions will allow all our team members to participate in the debugging process continuously, thereby enhancing the overall team members' understanding of the project and ensuring that they do not forget the progress of the project. This cycle aims to be implemented whenever there are new changes or functional additions to our website.

We made it a requirement to distinguish whether our test was successful or unsuccessful. We provide an example of the requirements (expected results) of the website by referring to the behavior-driven development (BDD) model. The definitions in these dictionaries aim to be as clear and objective as possible to reduce confusion. As a result, these conditions (expected results) aim to help provide the tester with clear acceptance criteria.
